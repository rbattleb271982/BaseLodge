PUT THIS IN AGENT

Run a focused “UI/UX Polish Sprint” across the app to improve clarity, spacing, and interaction quality without changing core logic or structure.

Goal:
Make the app feel more polished, intentional, and mobile-friendly using quick, high-impact improvements. Do not redesign the product or change architecture.

---

## Scope

Apply these improvements across:

- Home
- Trips
- Friend Trips tab
- Ideas
- Friends
- Trip detail page

---

## 1. Mobile spacing & layout (4.1)

- Ensure consistent vertical spacing between sections (no cramped or overly large gaps)
- Standardize padding inside cards/rows
- Ensure touch targets are comfortably tappable (minimum ~44px height)
- Align text consistently (headers, subtext, metadata)
- Remove any visual clutter or unnecessary separators

---

## 2. Text hierarchy & clarity (4.3)

- Ensure clear hierarchy:
  - Primary (destination / title)
  - Secondary (dates, names)
  - Tertiary (status like “going”, “considering”)
- Reduce visual noise:
  - avoid repeating the same information in multiple lines
- Ensure labels are consistent across the app (e.g., “going”, “considering”)

---

## 3. CTA clarity (4.3)

- Ensure buttons look like buttons (especially “Request to Join”)
- Make CTA states visually distinct:
  - default
  - pending (“Request Sent”)
  - accepted (“You’re Going”)
- Remove any ambiguous clickable areas that don’t look tappable

---

## 4. Navigation clarity (4.4 lite)

- Ensure tab labels are clear and consistent:
  - Home
  - Trips
  - Ideas
  - Friends
  - Profile
- Ensure sub-tabs (like Friend Trips) are clearly labeled and don’t imply action
- Make active tab states visually obvious

---

## 5. Feedback & interaction polish (4.3)

- Add subtle feedback for taps:
  - hover/active states (if available)
- Ensure buttons don’t feel “dead” when tapped
- Ensure loading states or empty states feel intentional (not broken)

---

## 6. Empty states

- Ensure empty states are:
  - neutral
  - not implying missing features
  - helpful but minimal

Example:
- “No friend trips yet” instead of “Nothing to join”

---

## Do NOT

- Do not change backend logic
- Do not add new features
- Do not redesign layouts
- Do not modify routing
- Do not touch data models

---

## Deliverables

- list of files updated
- summary of UI improvements made
- confirmation no functionality was changed
- any quick recommendations for next UX improvements

---

## Guidance

- prioritize mobile-first feel
- keep everything clean and minimal
- prefer small, consistent improvements over big changes
