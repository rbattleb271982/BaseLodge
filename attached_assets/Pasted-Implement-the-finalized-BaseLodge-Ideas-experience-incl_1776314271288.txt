## 1. IDEAS TAB (MAIN SCREEN)

### Header

Title:
Ideas

Subhead:
Where your plans and your friends line up.

Do not change this copy.

---

### Structure

Render a vertically stacked list of idea cards.

Rules:
- First card can be a featured “shared availability” card
- Remaining cards are standard cards
- Final card can be a “shared pull” (no date)

---

### Featured Card (Shared Availability)

Structure:

Kicker (small caps):
AN OPENING

Headline:
A four-day window

Sub-line:
With {FirstName}

Date line:
June 16 – June 19 · Telluride

Supporting text:
On both your lists. Passes align.

CTA:
Plan trip →

Rules:
- Headline and “With {Name}” should be on separate lines
- Keep tone observational, not suggestive
- Only show this card if strong overlap exists

---

### Standard Cards (Friend Trips)

Structure:

Date:
May 30 – June 3

Headline:
Join {FirstName} in {Destination}

Supporting text:
Covered on your Ikon pass.

CTA:
Join trip →

Rules:
- Use first name only
- Do not include last name here
- Keep copy neutral

---

### Shared Pull Card (No Dates)

Structure:

Headline:
Telluride keeps coming up

Supporting text:
Three of you have it wishlisted.

CTA:
Suggest dates →

Rules:
- No date displayed
- This should feel lighter and lower priority

---

### Name Handling (Ideas Tab)

- Default: first name only
- If duplicate names exist:
  - Use first name + last initial (e.g., Alex T.)
- Do not show full names on this screen

## 4. EMPTY STATE (IDEAS TAB)

When no ideas exist:

Header:
Ideas

Subhead:
Where your plans and your friends line up.

Divider

Main text:
Nothing lined up this week.

Supporting text:
Set availability or add a few resorts to your list to give it something to work with.

Actions (text links, not buttons):
Set availability →
Build your list →

Rules:
- No exclamation marks
- No “we’re working on it”
- Maintain calm, editorial tone

---

## 5. GENERAL DESIGN RULES

- Keep spacing generous
- Use serif for headlines, sans-serif for supporting text
- Use subtle dividers only
- Avoid badges, icons, or heavy UI elements
- No emoji
- No “AI” language
- No behavioral or predictive phrasing

Tone rule:
Only state what is true.
Do not interpret or suggest behavior.
