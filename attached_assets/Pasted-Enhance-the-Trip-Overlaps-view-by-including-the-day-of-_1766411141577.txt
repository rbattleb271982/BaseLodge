Enhance the Trip Overlaps view by including the day of the week in overlap sentences, while maintaining clear single-day vs multi-day date formatting.

This is a display and copy update only. Do not change overlap logic or database schema.

⸻

SECTION 1: Trip Overlaps copy format
	1.	Sentence structure
In the Trip Overlaps tab, display each overlap as a single human-readable sentence:

“You overlap with {First Name} {Last Name} on {Day, Date or Day–Day, Date Range} at {Resort}.”

Examples:
	•	“You overlap with Brook Eriksson on Mon, Jan 20 at Alta.”
	•	“You overlap with Sage Ortiz on Fri–Mon, Feb 14–Feb 17 at Eldora.”

⸻

SECTION 2: Name rules
	2.	Friend name display

	•	Always show first + last name when available
	•	If last name is missing, fall back to first name only
	•	Do not use usernames or emails

⸻

SECTION 3: Date + day formatting rules (important)
	3.	Single-day overlap
If start_date == end_date:

	•	Show the day of week and date once
	•	Format: “Mon, Jan 20”
	•	Do NOT repeat the date

Example:
“You overlap with Brook Eriksson on Mon, Jan 20 at Alta.”
	4.	Multi-day overlap
If start_date != end_date:

	•	Show day-of-week range and date range
	•	Format: “Fri–Mon, Feb 14–Feb 17”

Example:
“You overlap with Sage Ortiz on Fri–Mon, Feb 14–Feb 17 at Eldora.”
	5.	Formatting consistency

	•	Use abbreviated day names (Mon, Tue, Wed, Thu, Fri, Sat, Sun)
	•	Use existing date formatting utilities where possible
	•	Do not include the year unless already standard elsewhere in the app

⸻

SECTION 4: Remove irrelevant metadata
	6.	Pass information

	•	Do not display pass type (Epic, Ikon, etc.) in Trip Overlaps
	•	Pass is not relevant context for understanding overlaps
