#16 - Profile Expansion: Equipment, Mountains Visited, Wish List Redesign

What & Why

Redesign three Profile-related pages to match the BaseLodge editorial system and shift from form-based UX to interaction-based UX.

Pages:
1. Equipment
2. Mountains Visited
3. Wish List

The current versions:
- rely on boxed containers
- feel form-heavy
- use outdated UI patterns
- are inconsistent with newer screens (Friends, Trips, Home, Invite)

This refactor:
- removes form UX
- introduces tap-based, instant-save interactions
- unifies visual language
- improves usability on mobile

This is a structural + UX upgrade, not just styling.

Use checkpoints sparingly.

--------------------------------------------------
GLOBAL RULES (ALL 3 PAGES)
--------------------------------------------------

- Use editorial layout:
  - back chevron (no "Back" text)
  - eyebrow (uppercase section label)
  - serif headline
  - optional 1-line subtext
- No BaseLodge wordmark on these pages
- No large grey card containers
- Use spacing + hairline dividers instead
- No shadows
- Warm background, bordeaux accents

- ALL interactions = instant save
  - no Save button anywhere on these pages

- Remove mountains:
  - tap "remove" → immediately removed
  - no confirmation modal

- Maintain bottom nav
